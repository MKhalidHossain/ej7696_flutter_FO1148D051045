# Voice Assistant Quick Guide

Use the Voice Assistant to answer quiz questions by speaking.

- The assistant reads the question first, then listens for your answer.
- Speak after the question is finished.
- You can tap the mic to speak again.

## How It Works

- Listen to the question.
- Say your answer or command clearly.
- The app will select an answer, move questions, or open review based on what you say.
- If you want to speak again, tap the mic button.

## Common Voice Commands

You can use simple commands like:

- Option A
- Select A
- Choose A
- Option B
- Option C
- Option D
- True
- False
- Next question
- Previous question
- Skip question
- Read question
- Repeat question
- Explain
- Flag
- Open review
- Submit quiz

## Answering Questions

For multiple choice questions, say the option you want.

- Say "Option A", "Select A", or "Choose A".
- You can also say "Option B", "Option C", or "Option D".

For true or false questions:

- Say "True".
- Say "False".

## Moving Between Questions

Use voice commands to move through the quiz.

- Say "Next question" to go forward.
- Say "Previous question" to go back.
- Say "Skip question" if you want to leave a question for later.

## Reading Again / Help

If you need to hear the question again:

- Say "Read question".
- Say "Repeat question".

If you need more help:

- Say "Explain".

## Review and Submit

Use review before final submission.

- Say "Flag" to mark a question for review.
- Say "Open review" to go to the review screen.
- On the quiz screen, "Submit quiz" opens the review screen.
- On the review screen, "Submit quiz" submits your final answers.

## Tips for Better Recognition

For best results:

- Speak clearly.
- Keep the phone close to your mouth.
- Use a normal speaking speed.
- In noisy places, use a headset or earphones with a microphone.
- Wait until the assistant finishes reading before speaking.

## What To Do If It Does Not Understand

If the assistant does not understand you:

- Tap the mic and try again.
- Speak more clearly and slowly.
- Move to a quieter place if possible.
- Keep the phone closer.
- Use headset or earphones in noisy places.
